Quaid-e-Azam(1876-1948)

About

Quaid-e-Azam, Muhammad Ali Jinnah, born on December 25, 1876, in Karachi, was the founder of Pakistan. He was a brilliant lawyer and a charismatic leader, instrumental in advocating for the rights of Muslims in the Indian subcontinent. Jinnah's vision and relentless efforts led to the creation of Pakistan on August 14, 1947. As the country's first Governor-General, he worked tirelessly to establish a stable and prosperous nation. Jinnah's principles of unity, faith, and discipline continue to inspire millions. He passed away on September 11, 1948, but his legacy as a visionary leader endures.
